The great outdoors

This is a list of our favourite hikes in Hong Kong and its outlying islands.

Victoria Peak

If there’s one hike that defines Hong Kong, it has to be Victoria Peak with its panoramic views of Hong Kong island and beyond. If you’re feeling lazy, you can always take the Peak Tram up or down.

Dragon's Back

Another famous hike on Hong Kong island, ending at the iconic Big Wave Bay if you fancy a surf or swim at the end of your adventure.

The Twins

One for the hard core hikers. Infamous for its nearly 1,400 step ascent, your exertion will be richly rewarded with spectacular views of the south side of Hong Kong island. Treat yourself to a well-earned beer at Stanley Market (see below) at the end.

Cheung Chau Island

A perfect combination of hiking, seafood dining, beach time and family fun. Cheung Chau is only a 30 minute ferry ride from Central piers, yet feels like a far-removed tropical island with palm trees and azure waters. It even has its very own pirate cave for the kids. Our favourite of Hong Kong’s outer islands.

Sunset Peak

A moderately difficult hike on Lantau island (the third highest peak in Hong Kong), but you will be richly rewarded with panoramic views of Hong Kong and its outer islands. Can be incorporated into a day trip to Lantau island.

A bit of culture

History Museum

Our favourite museum with a permanent exhibition of the history of Hong Kong (starting from the Big Bang (!) all the way to the handover).

Heritage Museum

For those who don’t mind travelling further afield to the New Territories, the Hong Kong Heritage Museum has a bit of everything: Chinese history, art and culture.

Cultural Centre

Just a short ferry ride across Victoria Harbour (which we also highly recommend) is the Hong Kong Cultural Centre in Tsim Sha Tsui, which has daily concerts and performances worth checking out.

Tai Kwun

Conveniently located in Central/Mid-Levels, Tai Kwun is a cultural hub with a focus on art and music. It is situated in the restored Central Police Station compound. You could easily spend an entire afternoon exploring the various exhibitions, cool restaurants/bars and shops. Nat also recommends their guided tour (booking in advance advisable).

Feeling peckish

We’re totally spoilt for choice when it comes to eating and drinking in Hong Kong. Here are some of our favourite places, but don’t just take our word for it - check out the many food blogs and reviews for other recommendations.

The ultimate food directory app to all of Hong Kong’s restaurants and cafes

Eat like a local

City Hall Maxim's Palace (old style trolleys filed with dim sum baskets), Joy & Joy(no-fuss, reasonably priced), Dim Sum Library (upscale, “innovative” dim sum options), Social Place (moderately priced “innovative” dim sum options), seafood on Lamma Island (e.g. Fu Kee or Rainbow (Rainbow provides its own transportation to Lamma island by ferry))

Romantic Dinner

Cafe Grey Deluxe (where we celebrated our first anniversary!), Limewood (overlooks the beach, perfect after hiking Violet Hill), Hutong (great views), Aulis (small, intimate, chef’s table), Francis (buzzy restaurant with Middle Eastern food).

Beach time

Hong Kong has so many fantastic beaches that it's difficult to come up with a definitive list. We would encourage you to get out and explore, but the below are some of our favourites.

Repulse Bay/ Deep Water Bay

A stone's throw from the reception venue, these are 2 of the most popular tourist beaches in Hong Kong and offer a variety of restaurants (eg the Pulse) and family friendly fun.

Sai Kung

If you fancy getting away from the crowds, this one is for you. About 45 mins by taxi from the city centre, Sai Kung offers empty beaches with turquoise waters and great waterfalls. A hike through Sai Kung national park followed by an afternoon at the beach, with freshly caught seafood for dinner, makes for a perfect day out.

Stanley

Located at the most southern point of Hong Kong island, Stanley is home to the famous Stanley markets where you can pick yourself up a bargain (including Jon's favourite dragon T-shirts) and a fresh coconut before hitting the beach. Kayaks and SUPs all available for hire.
